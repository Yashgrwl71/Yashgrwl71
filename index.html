About
From blueprints
to balance sheets.

I started out as a mechanical engineer in India, then spent four years catching fraud patterns at Amazon and Goldman Sachs before deciding I wanted to sit on the investing side of the table, not just the risk-control side of it. That's what brought me to the University of Utah for a dual MBA and MS in Finance.

Since then I've built my work around three things: public equities, healthcare and medical device investing, and impact investing. At the Student Investment Fund, I helped run a $1.1M portfolio, picked Uber at $69 (up 40%+ since), and built a commodities hedge that beat the S&P 500 through a market drawdown. At Sorenson Impact and the Center for Medical Innovation, I sourced and diligenced early-stage healthcare and edtech deals and sized medtech markets worth $395M+. Outside of school, I helped take a medical device idea to a filed patent and a licensing deal, and built the financial model behind a D2C apparel brand's first funding round.

What I'm actually good at: taking a rough idea — a stock, a startup, a device, a strategy — and turning it into a model with real assumptions, a defensible number, and a recommendation I'd stand behind in a room.
