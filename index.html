My Strategic Perspectives for URS

Based on my deep dive into how private markets are shifting, here are the key areas I believe a long-term investor like URS should really focus on.

1

Positioning Real Assets for the Energy and Digital Shift

I see real assets, especially infrastructure and energy, as crucial for URS. They offer solid protection against inflation and provide dependable income. With AI and data centers driving a massive surge in power demand (set to nearly triple by 2030), there’s huge potential in energy infrastructure like HVDC (High-Voltage Direct Current) transmission and Green Data Centers. In the mining sector, focusing not just on extraction but also on processing and recycling of critical minerals like copper and lithium offers direct exposure to the energy transition. Traditional fossil fuel assets can still generate cash, but they need clear decarbonization plans or a defined role as transition fuels.

2

Sharpening Private Equity Strategy through Thematic Exposure

My research shows the private equity landscape keeps evolving, especially with exit challenges and valuation shifts. I believe URS can boost its private equity returns by targeting managers who really specialize in long-term themes. Think AI, specialized software, healthcare technology, and climate solutions. These areas have strong fundamentals and powerful tailwinds. Partnering with the best GPs in these spaces would let URS align with big structural shifts, aiming for unique returns while managing risk and fees smartly.

3

Navigating Global Markets with Focused Investments

I see clear opportunities for URS to build more exposure in certain global markets, but we need to be very focused and intentional. Demographics, digital adoption, and a growing middle class are fueling rapid growth across regions like Asia, Latin America, and parts of Africa. Instead of broad allocations, I'd look at specific sectors like infrastructure, fintech, and logistics, where long-term fundamentals are undeniably strong.

4

Investing in the Circular Economy and Resource Efficiency

Beyond the energy transition, there's a massive, multi-decade investment opportunity in the shift toward a circular economy and extreme resource efficiency. This isn't just about 'being green'; it's about fundamentally rethinking how industries operate and how we use resources to minimize waste. This includes advanced recycling that truly transforms waste, sustainable food systems that use resources smarter, industrial decarbonization solutions for heavy industries, and smart water management for a world facing increasing scarcity. This area offers significant long-term gains by tackling core global challenges that will drive huge investment and innovation.
